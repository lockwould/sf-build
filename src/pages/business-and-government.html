Take the lead in creating a sustainable future

Research shows that today’s consumers are more environmentally conscious than ever:

91%

of global consumers expect companies to address environmental issues in their business practices.1

73%

of consumers ages 15-35 are increasingly willing to pay more for products and services from companies committed to sustainability.2

74%

of Americans believe that the government should do “whatever it takes to protect the environment.”3

Any business or governmental organization doing its part to move toward a clean energy future should be proud to demonstrate that commitment. That’s pretty hard to do, however, with rooftop-mounted solar hidden out of sight or other common sources of clean energy. That’s where smartflower comes in. Its can’t-miss design makes smartflower the perfect way to generate clean energy while showing your customers and community what a forward-thinking enterprise looks like.

Powered by smartflower

Tourism

The Botanical Gardens in Madrid, Spain offer children and teens an outdoor, learning space called Nature’s Classroom. smartflower produces power needed to run activities throughout the day and into the evening

Education

At the University of Applied Sciences Kufstein, smartflower powers the café in the student union as well as walkway lighting for night time visitors.

Agriculture

Smartflower is the perfect solution to power irrigation and lighting for both decorative and working gardens, whether at home, outside your office building or on a farm.

Hopsitality

Restaurant Fond Rose in Caluire-et-Cuire, France harvests smartflower’s energy to create clean recipes inspired by organic and fresh ingredients from local vendors. smartflower reflects the restaurant’s all-natural cuisine while promoting a more sustainable future.

Business

Thanks to smartflower, a car dealer in Europe reduces their environment footprint and saves on its electricity bill by using the sun’s energy to help power their brightly lit showroom and expansive lot.

Government

Positioned front and center of the county administration building ensure that the county’s citizens are reminded every day of the county’s commitment to a net zero carbon footprint and provide a robust complement to the building’s transition to being powered by renewable energy sources.
